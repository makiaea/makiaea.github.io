The book/a gateway to knowledge The mirror/a gateway to reflection The sword/a gateway to action

  • The book symbolises the gateway to knowledge.
  • The mirror or drum symbolises the gateway to reflection. Together the book and the mirror symbolise the gateway to wisdom.
  • The sword symbolises the gateway to action. Together the book, the mirror, and the sword symbolise the gateway to responsible living.

the book of antiquity

  • or, the word, or the message arrives how or what do we need to learn? The book/a gateway to knowledge
  • How much of past can we truly learn? The past is gone, and thousands of years of sarcomata remain. But we can, with imagination, learn something from the past. In part 1, "the book", we will deal with ways to find and absorb knowledge.
  • Current textbooks are too absorbed by authority and repetition. We assume you can grasp a concept immediately, or else know to ponder over something and come back at it.

the mirror of eternity

  • or, the signal drum, or the call to action
  • how or what do we need to think? The mirror/a gateway to reflection
  • In part 2 of the book, "the mirror", we will be looking at future trends and appropriate methods for dealing with them. Here we deal with ways to interpret, understand and use knowledge.
  • Possible topics include responsibility, evolution, ecocide as crime, family structure, peak oil.

the sword of serenity

  • or, the battle
  • how or what to we need to act? The sword/a gateway to action
  • The principle of understanding and working with the flow of natural processes helps us make good use of our resources. It is like when your hand is caught by a cat's claws when you play together — if you know to move towards the cat's paws, rather than away. Moving towards means that the claws can no longer grip you, since they are pointed towards the cat, while moving away helps strengthen the grip and may even help to cause damage if moving away sufficiently quickly. Similar types of principles are espoused in aikido and zen meditation. We will be approaching some everyday processes from this point of view in part 3 of the book, "the sword".
